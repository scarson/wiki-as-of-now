## Findings

1. **The referent-context feature works** (primary question of this batch): pronoun/definite-subject claims now produce entity-specific queries (M72 = direct evidence; M109's queries even resolved "vehicles" → "M109A7"). Zero `[placeholder]` queries anywhere.
2. **advisorySupport overclaims on related-background cards** — 3 of 12 human-judged pairs (M72 funding card, TSMC N3E card, Blackjack insitu product page) marked advisory=true for cards that don't resolve the claim. Matches the San Jose–Gilroy pattern from handoff-b; now quantified (25% overclaim rate on this sample) for the calibration item.
3. **Source-quality gap (prefer-official):** the 3 nm pack grounded on baike.baidu.com while the official TSMC pressroom page exists (it IS the gold evidence). The per-host cap allowed both cards from the same weak host. Candidate improvement: host-quality weighting or an official-domain preference in triage presentation — needs design thought (the show-your-work guardrail limits how much the tool may hide).
4. **Retrieval can fail with perfect queries** (M109 `no_proposals`): good queries ≠ good hits; the Brave result set for procurement-total questions was thin. Worth a second batch data point before designing anything.
5. **Verify layer earns its keep online**: the M72 pack's drop of the gold gatesmri.org page for `quote_not_found` shows the deterministic verbatim check rejecting a real-but-unquotable proposal rather than shipping a fabricated quote.
6. **Operational: Workers AI latency episodes are a real availability mode** for the research path (60+ min of consistent >60 s Gemma latency). The queue's retry/DLQ semantics handled it cleanly and cost-free. If episodes recur, options are a larger `callTimeoutMs`, the escalation model, or DLQ-replay tooling — decide on evidence, not this single episode.
