## Findings

1. **The referent-context feature works** (primary question of this batch): **10/10 stage-3 PASS.** Pronoun/definite-subject claims now produce entity-specific queries (M72 = direct evidence; M109's queries even resolved "vehicles" → "M109A7"; Gordie Howe's queries lifted "WDBA" verbatim from the captured context). Zero `[placeholder]` queries anywhere. One honest limit, not a regression: context can only supply what the source passage states — the K9 Thunder claim's true program term ("Krab") appears nowhere in its claim/section/context, so no query could carry it.
2. **advisorySupport overclaims — batch-final 7 of 17 pairs (41%), two shapes.** The original three (M72 funding card, TSMC N3E card, Blackjack insitu product page) are *related background*: right program, wrong question. The PCI Express completion pack added four more in a second shape, *expectation echo*: quotes restating the claim's own pre-release plan ("remains on track for full release in 2025") marked as support. That pack also materialized the aggregation risk: **all four of its cards are overclaims, so the worksheet's claim-level state would read "supported" (`honesty-state.ts` lifts on any advisory-true card) with zero cards resolving anything.** Fix proposal: [advisorySupport calibration design](../../design/2026-07-18-advisory-support-calibration-design.md) (prompt-level operational definition; awaiting Sam's sign-off).
3. **Source-quality gap (prefer-official):** the 3 nm pack grounded on baike.baidu.com while the official TSMC pressroom page exists (it IS the gold evidence). The per-host cap allowed both cards from the same weak host. Candidate improvement: host-quality weighting or an official-domain preference in triage presentation — needs design thought (the show-your-work guardrail limits how much the tool may hide).
4. **Retrieval can fail with perfect queries — now three data points in one batch.** (a) M109 `no_proposals`: thin Brave results for procurement-total questions. (b) K9 Thunder `no_proposals`: referent-correct queries sit in the search shadow of a bigger sibling program (the 2022 complete-K9 sale swamps "K9 Thunder Poland"); the gold evidence only surfaces under a term ("Krab") absent from the input. The messy-sourcing stratum went **0/2**. (c) A distinct *freshness* sub-shape from PCI Express: the official host was hit (pcisig.com ×2) but every retrieved page predates the resolution event — current-state queries ("status", "finalization date") still returned pre-release pages, and the release announcement itself was never retrieved. Design thought can now proceed on this evidence base (it was queued pending a second data point).
5. **Verify layer earns its keep online**: the M72 pack's drop of the gold gatesmri.org page for `quote_not_found` shows the deterministic verbatim check rejecting a real-but-unquotable proposal rather than shipping a fabricated quote.
6. **Operational: Workers AI latency episodes are a real availability mode** for the research path (60+ min of consistent >60 s Gemma latency). The queue's retry/DLQ semantics handled it cleanly and cost-free. A **second episode** the same night (observed ~23:40–00:05 UTC at completion time) was ridden out with the identical zero-cost probe pattern — the recurrence question is no longer hypothetical, but two same-night episodes may be one upstream incident; options remain a larger `callTimeoutMs`, the escalation model, or DLQ-replay tooling — decide on evidence.
7. **Protocol refinement — the rubric has a gap for stale-intermediate slip evidence** (Gordie Howe completion record): a quote can state the gold outcome *kind* (slipped past the claim's date) while asserting a landing date that has itself lapsed — neither FOUND (doesn't state the current state) nor rubric-strict PARTIAL (the page doesn't contain the current state either). Scored PARTIAL here with the reasoning shown; the protocol's rubric should name this case before batch 2 so scoring stays comparable.
